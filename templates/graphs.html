Understanding the Graphs

MSE Loss

Mean Squared Error (MSE) measures the average squared difference between predicted and actual values. Lower values indicate better model performance. Ideally, both training and validation loss should decrease and stabilize.

MAE

Mean Absolute Error (MAE) measures the average absolute difference between predicted and actual values. Unlike MSE, it's not sensitive to outliers and provides a more intuitive measure of error magnitude.
