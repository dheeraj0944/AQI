Health Protection

{{ bucket }}
{% if bucket in ['GOOD', 'Satisfactory'] %}

Minimal health precautions needed.

  • No special protective measures needed for the general population
  • People with severe respiratory conditions may still want to monitor their symptoms
  • Good time for outdoor exercise to improve lung capacity and overall health
{% elif bucket == 'Moderate' %}

Some health precautions recommended for sensitive groups.

  • People with respiratory conditions should carry their medication when outdoors
  • Consider wearing a mask if you experience irritation or work in high-traffic areas
  • Stay hydrated to help your body filter airborne particles
  • Monitor air quality updates if planning prolonged outdoor activities
{% elif bucket in ['Poor', 'Very Poor'] %}

Health protection measures strongly recommended.

  • Wear N95 or equivalent masks when outdoors for extended periods
  • Use air purifiers at home and keep windows closed during peak pollution hours
  • People with asthma should keep rescue inhalers readily accessible
  • Consider relocating outdoor activities to early morning when pollution levels might be lower
  • Install air quality monitoring apps for real-time updates
{% else %}

Serious health protection measures required.

  • Wear high-efficiency particulate air (HEPA) filter masks if going outside is unavoidable
  • Create a clean air room at home with air purifiers running continuously

  • Consider temporary relocation if air quality doesn't improve
  • Seek medical attention immediately for breathing difficulties or chest pain

Additional Long-term Recommendations

Improve Indoor Air Quality

Use air purifiers with HEPA filters, incorporate indoor plants, and ensure proper ventilation during cleaner air periods.

Reduce Personal Contribution

Limit vehicle use, avoid burning waste, and choose eco-friendly products to help improve overall air quality.

Health Monitoring

Regular check-ups for respiratory health, especially for children, elderly, and those with pre-existing conditions.

Stay Informed

Monitor air quality indexes regularly and plan activities accordingly, especially during seasonal pollution peaks.
